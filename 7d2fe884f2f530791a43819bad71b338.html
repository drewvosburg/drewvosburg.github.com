ShopFresh

Concept for Table XI

ShopFresh is a cost-effective and comprehensive grocery delivery service focused on helping people find something good to eat.

By serving up recipes and allowing customers to get whole recipes delivered, ShopFresh can function as an on-demand meal kit service, or customers can shop by department like a traditional store.

The process of grocery shopping can vary from customer to customer. Meal planning companies like Blue Apron and Hello Fresh indicate that there is also a meal planning gap for a lot of people. Meal planning is a chore, and it's hard to make sure you get every ingredient you need.

My approach for solving this problem started with a brief survey of people I know who regularly grocery shop, as well as those in my network who have used grocery delivery service. I was then able to identify two shopping and planning styles around which I created personas:

Dawson - Meal ideas and recommendations

  • Middle Class
  • Some college experience
  • Mid twenties
  • Likes to cook
  • Interested in trying new foods
  • Thrifty, trying to save as much as possible

Victoria - Straightfoward shopping experience

  • Upper middle class
  • Bachelor's degree
  • Early thirties
  • Uninterested in making a meal
  • Not looking for recommendations
  • Mainly focused on value and finding things easily

From this base framework, I created a core flow that includes both a traditional shopping experience and a recipe-centric flow. When shopping, produce and other items suggest recipes that you might try using that item. You can then get a whole recipe delivered, excluding the ingredients you already have.
